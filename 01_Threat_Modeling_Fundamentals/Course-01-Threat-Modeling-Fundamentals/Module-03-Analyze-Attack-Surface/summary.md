In this module, I learned how important it is to choose the right level of detail when creating threat models. Too little detail can miss risks, and too much detail can make everything confusing.

I understood that data flow diagrams can be created in layers, starting from a high-level overview and then going deeper step by step. This layered approach makes it easier to understand complex systems.

Each layer gives a different perspective. The system layer shows the big picture, while deeper layers break things into smaller processes and components.

This helped me realize that threat modeling is not just about drawing diagrams, but about providing the right amount of context for better decision-making.
